Impossible contexts

In the void of space, where there is no air, temperature, or medium to transmit touch, the idea of feeling becomes paradoxical. How could one "feel" the absence of everything? Would the lack itself become a sensation?

Imagine a fully digital existence, where beings are lines of code and binary data. How could "feeling" exist without physical senses or emotions? Could algorithms simulate what it means to feel?

In a realm where time doesn't flow, feelings as fleeting experiences would seem contradictory. Without a beginning or end, how could a feeling arise, evolve, or fade?

Can there be feeling without someone or something to feel it? Could a feeling exist independently, like a wandering entity, waiting to attach itself to a conscious mind?
