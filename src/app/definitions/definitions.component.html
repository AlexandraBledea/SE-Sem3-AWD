Definitions

PHYSICAL SENSATION

The capacity to perceive and experience sensations such as touch, temperature, pain, or motion, typically though the skin. It encompasses the awareness of physical conditions in the body or external stimuli.


Example: She experienced a soothing feeling of warmth from the sun on her skin

Think of emotions as the sparks—they’re automatic, universal responses to stimuli, like joy, fear, or anger. Feelings, on the other hand, are how we make sense of those sparks, shaping them into something personal and conscious. For example, fear is the emotion, but saying “I feel scared” is the feeling—a reflection of how we interpret and experience it.
